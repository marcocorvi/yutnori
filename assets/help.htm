Y U T N O R I

Yut Nori is a traditinal korean game.
Use "Back" button to quit Yut-Nori.

There are two ways to play:

  1. you play against Android.
  2. you play against your friend.

THE RULES OF YUT NORI

There are two players.
Each player has four pawns (mal).
The Yut Nori board has 29 stations.
At the beginning all the pawns are on the START at the bottom left.
You move the pawns on the board starting from the start station, indicated by the black L-arrow, and go around the board counterclockwise, until they pass beyond the fourth corner and are placed in the HOME at the bottom right.
If a pawn stops at a corner at the next move you can decide to move it through the shortcut diagonal.

The game is won by the player who completes the board course with all the four pawns first.

Therefore there are five routes to go through the board.


To move the pawn the players throw the Yut Nori sticks (yut) in turn.
They move the pawns (mal) by the score they get:

  • one stick up (do): move by one step
  • two sticks up (gae): move by two steps
  • three sticks up (geol): move by three steps
  • four sticks up (yut): move by four steps
  • all sticks down (mo): move by five steps

When a player scores yut (4) or mo (5) he gets another throw. Therefore there is no limit to the number of scores a player can get before he moves. The scores can be played separatedly, each given to a pawn, but a score cannot be split among two pawns.

If a pawn lands on a station occupied by the other player's pawns these are sent back to the START, and the player gets another throw of the sticks.

If a pawn lands on a station occupied by another pawn of the same player, the pawns form a group and travel together from that point on.

SPECIAL GAME RULES

Sometimes special rules are added to the traditional Yut-Nori to make the game more interesting.
One, two, or three sticks are marked with a BACK sign. When only one stick is up with the BACK mark, that is a back-do and the pawns move according to a special rule.

By default the app starts playing the traditional Yut-Nori, with no special rule.
You choose to play with a special rule and the number of sticks with the BACK mark, before starting the game.
The default number of sticks with the BACK mark is an app setting, as well as the default app rule.

The rule you are playing with is shown in the title bar.

With the Seoul rule, a back-do moves a pawn directly from START to the Seoul station, the center of the board. If there are no pawns at START, it is a normal do and the pawns move one step forward.

With the Busan rule, a back-do moves a pawn from START to the Busan station, the first corner of the board. If there are no pawns at START, it is a normal do and the pawns move one step forward.

For the other special rules pawns on the board move one step backward with a back-do. If the pawns are at the third or fourth corner (last station), or at the center station, the player chooses to which station the pawns move back.
In order to clear up the question of where to move a pawn at the first station (Do station), or what to do when there is no pawn on the board there are four ways to play a back-do: Back-Do, Skip Do, Do Spot, Do Cage.

With the plain Back-Do pawns at the Do-station go to START. If there are no pawns on the board a back-do has the effect of a normal do and a pawn is moved from START to the Do-station.

Skip Do is like the plain Back-Do, but if the player has no pawns on the board he skips the turn.

In Do spot pawns at the Do-station move to the last station with a back-do. If the player has no pawn on the board a back-do is treated as a normal Do, and a pawn is moved from START to the Do-station.

In Do cage pawns at the Do-station go to START. If there are no pawns on the board, a pawn goes from START to the CAGE, underneath START. A pawn in the CAGE stays there until another back-do is thrown, at which occasion it goes to the last station.

PLAYING WITH ANDROID

When you open Yut-Nori, a menu appears. You choose "Play against Android", the number of back yuts, and the game rule. If you do not choose any rule the traditional Yutnori game is played. Android's pawns are RED. Your pawns are BLUE, and you start first.

When it is your turn to throw the sticks, a yut picture appears at the bottom and "THROW" is displayed in the title. Tap on the yut picture to throw the sticks. The outcome is shown above the board.

After you have thrown the sticks, the title becomes "MOVE". To move a pawn (or a group of pawns) tap on the pawn you want to move (or on the pawns at to bottom left). They are highlighted with a blue background. Then tap on the station you want to move it to. To move it to the "home" station tap at the bottom right.

When it is Android's turn, the title becomes red and it shows "WAIT".

When the game is over the title displays the winner and a dialog tells you who won.

Special rules
To do a Seoul or a Busan move, tap the pawns in the START.
To skip your turn, tap the screen in the bottom middle.
To move the pawn from the Do-station to the last station or to START, first tap the Do station, then the destination.
To move a pawn from START to the Do Cage tap START or the Do Cage.
To move the pawn from the Do Cage to the final station, tap the Do Cage.

There are three different play logics for Android. The choice is one of the app settings.

PLAYING WITH YOUR FRIEND

You and your friend must have your Androids paired on bluetooth.

Open your Yut-Nori's.
In the start dialog one of you chooses the number of back yuts and the game rule. Next he taps "Wait for a friend's Yut-Nori request" and waits. The other too chooses the number of back yuts and the game rule he wants to play with. Then he taps "Ask a friend to play together".
When the list of paired devices appears, he selects the friend's device, and taps Attach. Android sends to the friend a message "... wants to play with you", where ... is the name of the Android that asked the connection.
The friend selects "Accept". Android sends messages to both of you "Connected to .... Let's decide who starts. Your color is RED/BLUE". You both must select "OK".

To decide who starts, you and your friend throw the yuts in turns (when the yut picture appears at the bottom). The outcome is shown above the board with a red and blue background according to your colors.
If your scores are equal you get to repeat the choice.
When the scores are different a message ("You start" or "Your friend starts") tells who starts.

Afterwards, playing is like playing with Android. The game is played according to the Yyutnori rules of the player that starts first.

When the game is over the dialog gives you an option to play another game. If one of you choose so, the winner starts the game and gets to throw the yuts first.

SETTINGS

  • Logic: you can choose among three playing logic for Android, or let the app pick one at random
  • Speed: the app delay between moves
  • Back Yuts: the default number of yuts with the back mark, 1, 2, or 3
  • Yutnori rule: default Yutnori rule
